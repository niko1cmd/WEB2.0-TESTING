FAQ

Common Questions on Limit Orders

A limit order is an instruction to buy or sell currency only when the market reaches a rate you've pre-set. The order stays active until your target rate is hit at which point it executes automatically or until the expiry date you specified. You set it and forget it.

If the market doesn't reach your target before the order expires, the order simply lapses no transaction is made and no fees are charged. Your advisor can help you reassess and reset at a revised target if needed.

Yes. You can run concurrent limit orders across 135+ currency pairs simultaneously. Your FinXtra advisor maintains full visibility of all active orders and keeps you informed of any market developments that may affect your targets.

Limit orders can be set for a specific date range from a single trading session to several months. For longer-horizon FX protection, your advisor may also recommend combining a limit order strategy with forward contracts.

A forward contract locks in a rate today for a future settlement date regardless of where the market moves. A limit order waits for the market to reach your desired rate before executing. Both protect you from adverse rate movements, but in different ways. Your advisor can help you determine which tool or combination fits your situation.
