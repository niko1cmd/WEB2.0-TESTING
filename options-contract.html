What Is an Options Contract?

The Right to Act.
Without the Duty to.

An FX options contract gives your business the right to exchange currency at a pre-agreed rate — but only if the rate is favourable when the contract matures. If the market moves in your favour instead, you simply let the option lapse and transact at the better live rate. You define your worst-case rate. Everything better than that is upside you keep.

Worst-Case Rate Defined

Your strike rate is the floor. The market can only go better from there — you're fully protected on the downside.

Keep the Upside

If the market improves beyond your strike rate, let the option lapse and transact at the better live rate instead.

135+ Currency Pairs

Structure options across all major and emerging market pairs — wherever your cross-border exposure exists.

Flexible Expiry Dates

Set the option window to match your payment schedule — from a few weeks to several months out.

Who Should Use Options Contracts?

Any business pricing international contracts, submitting tenders in foreign currencies, or managing uncertain payment timelines can benefit from options contract protection.

CFOs & Finance Directors

Protect foreign currency payables and receivables without giving up the ability to benefit from rate improvements. Ideal when the timing or certainty of a payment is still in flux.

Founders & Business Owners

Pricing international contracts or submitting tenders in foreign currencies? An option protects your margin at the rate you used to build the quote — without locking you in if the deal falls through.

Finance Teams

Manage FX risk on budget cycles, supplier contracts, or revenue repatriation with a flexible instrument that adapts to your cashflow — not the other way around.
